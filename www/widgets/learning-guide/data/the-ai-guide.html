CHAPTER 1

When Choosing to Use AI

Artificial "Intelligence" (AI) is surrounded by a lot of hype, and knowing when that hype is warranted requires an understanding of how it works. But understanding AI isn't a requirement for creatively coding online, what matters most is understanding the Web itself (You can click "back" to access all those notes). If you are curious about AI though, and how best to incorporate it into your creative practice, these notes are designed to help you make informed decisions about when, how, and whether to use this technology in your work.

CHAPTER 2
AI as Coding Assistants

It’s tempting, with coding assistants at hand, to “vibe-code” your way to something interesting. But your imagination is bounded by your understanding of the medium. If we turn to AI coding assistants too early and too often, our ideas become constrained by what a chatbot suggests, and while they're very profficient coders, they're not very creative thinkers.

That said, the future of coding will likely involve some degree of AI-assistance. To learn to use these systems in ways that benefit us, extending our capabilities and supporting our creativity rather than undermining our learning and limiting our ideas, we've created a widget to help you craft the most effective prompts.





CHAPTER 3
Going Deeper with APIs

As explained in chapter 1, an app's graphical user interface (GUI) controls how you interact with the model behind it, limiting not only what you can do, but what you think to do, often in ways you might not realize. When we interact with an AI model directly, as code, we expand the creative possibilities beyond what the desginers of an app's GUI imagined for us. The first step we can take in that direction is to communicate directly with a model's "application programming interface" or API.

We've explained that netnet is a classical AI system, meaning everything it says was written by hand, nothing is synthesized by an LLM. However, you can connect netnet to an API having it act as a conduit or medium for your chosen LLM running in a datacenter.


This widget will reveal what it means for a writing/coding app to be "AI powered" by making that connection transparent. netnet will guide you through the process, allowing you to make some decisions (like choosing your preferred LLM provider and model settings) while making others for you (setting system prompt and response schema) to ensure we integrate LLMs in a way that serves our learning goals.

CHAPTER 5
AI as Art: Crafting Neural Networks from Scratch

In earlier chapters we talked about how neural networks learn from data rather than following hand-written rules, but we kept things at a high level. In this chapter, we're going to look under the hood and walk through the process of creating a neural network from the ground up. The goal isn't to become machine learning engineers, it's to develop a grounded understanding of this technology that informs our creative instincts when critiquing, embracing or otherwise working with AI.
