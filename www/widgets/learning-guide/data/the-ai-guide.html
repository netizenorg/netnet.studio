CHAPTER 1

When Choosing to Use AI

Artificial “Intelligence” (AI) is surrounded by a lot of hype, and knowing when that hype is warranted requires an understanding of how it works. This guide is meant to build that understanding, but it's not required, you can click "back" and jump straight into HTML (the Web existed long before all this hype after all). But, if you are curious about AI, one of the best ways to learn is through hands-on experimentation. Before you do, it’s worth considering which tools best align with your values.

CHAPTER 2
LLMs as Coding Assistants

It’s tempting, with coding assistants at hand, to “vibe-code” your way to something interesting. But your imagination is bounded by your understanding of the medium. If we turn to AI coding assistants too early and too often, our ideas become constrained by what a chatbot suggests, and while they're very profficient coders, they're not very creative thinkers.

Than said, the future of coding is likely going to involve some sort of AI-assistance, in order to make sure we learn to use these systems in ways that benefit us, extending our capabilities and supporting our creativity, rather than undermining our learning and limiting our ideas, we've created a widget to help you craft the most effective prompts.





CHAPTER 3
The APIs Behind the Chat Bots

As explained in chapter 1, an app's graphical user interface (GUI) controls what you can/can't do with the model, which in turn limits what you can to do with it, often in ways you might not realize. When we interact with an AI model directly, as a function, we expand the creative possabilities beyond what the desginers of an app's GUI imagined for us. One step we can take to peak beyond these limts is to communicate directly with a model's "application programming interface" or API.

We've explained that netnet is a classical AI system, meaning everything it says was written by hand, nothing is synthesized by an LLM. However, you can connect netnet to an API having it act as a conduit or medium for the LLM running in a datacenter.


This widget will guide you through the process of querying LLM APIs directly, super charging netnet's ability to help debug our code, while encouraging best practices for new creative coders.
